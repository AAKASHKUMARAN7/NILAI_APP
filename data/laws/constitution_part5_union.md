id: art37
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 37 - Application of the principles contained in this Part
part: Part IV
keywords: directive principles, non-justiciable, governance, fundamental, duty of state, policy
---

The provisions contained in this Part shall not be enforceable by any court, but the principles therein laid down are nevertheless fundamental in the governance of the country and it shall be the duty of the State to apply these principles in making laws. The Supreme Court in Minerva Mills v. Union of India (1980) held that Directive Principles and Fundamental Rights together form the "core" of the Constitution and must be read harmoniously. Courts have used DPSPs to expand the scope of Fundamental Rights, particularly Article 21.

id: art40
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 40 - Organisation of village panchayats
part: Part IV
keywords: village panchayat, self-government, local governance, gram sabha, decentralization, 73rd amendment
---

The State shall take steps to organise village panchayats and endow them with such powers and authority as may be necessary to enable them to function as units of self-government. This directive principle was given constitutional force by the 73rd Amendment (1992) which added Part IX to the Constitution, mandating the establishment of Panchayati Raj institutions at village, intermediate and district levels.

---
id: art42
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 42 - Provision for just and humane conditions of work and maternity relief
part: Part IV
keywords: just conditions, humane, work, maternity, relief, workers, women, labour, pregnancy
---

The State shall make provision for securing just and humane conditions of work and for maternity relief. This has led to legislations like the Maternity Benefit Act 1961 (amended 2017 to provide 26 weeks paid leave), the Factories Act, the Minimum Wages Act, and the Occupational Safety, Health and Working Conditions Code, 2020. The Supreme Court has read this with Article 21 to expand the right to dignified working conditions.

---
id: art43a
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 43A - Participation of workers in management of industries
part: Part IV
keywords: workers, management, participation, industries, industrial democracy, labour
---

The State shall take steps, by suitable legislation or in any other way, to secure the participation of workers in the management of undertakings, establishments or other organisations engaged in any industry. This was inserted by the 42nd Amendment (1976) and reflects the principle of industrial democracy.

---
id: art47
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 47 - Duty of the State to raise the level of nutrition and the standard of living and to improve public health
part: Part IV
keywords: nutrition, standard of living, public health, intoxicating drinks, drugs, prohibition, health
---

The State shall regard the raising of the level of nutrition and the standard of living of its people and the improvement of public health as among its primary duties and, in particular, the State shall endeavour to bring about prohibition of the consumption except for medicinal purposes of intoxicating drinks and of drugs which are injurious to health. The Supreme Court in PUCL v. Union of India has read this with Article 21 to recognize the right to food as a fundamental right.

---
id: art48
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 48 - Organisation of agriculture and animal husbandry
part: Part IV
keywords: agriculture, animal husbandry, cattle, cow slaughter, modern scientific lines, breeds, prohibition
---

The State shall endeavour to organise agriculture and animal husbandry on modern and scientific lines and shall, in particular, take steps for preserving and improving the breeds, and prohibiting the slaughter, of cows and calves and other milch and draught cattle. Several state laws on cow slaughter prohibition derive their basis from this article.

---
id: art49
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 49 - Protection of monuments and places and objects of national importance
part: Part IV
keywords: monuments, national importance, historical, artistic, archaeological, protection, heritage, ASI
---

It shall be the obligation of the State to protect every monument or place or object of artistic or historic interest, declared by or under law made by Parliament to be of national importance, from spoliation, disfigurement, destruction, removal, disposal or export, as the case may be. The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) and the Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act implement this directive.

---
id: art51
category: Directive Principles
title: Article 51 - Promotion of international peace and security
part: Part IV
keywords: international peace, security, nations, treaties, arbitration, disputes, foreign policy
---

The State shall endeavour to (a) promote international peace and security; (b) maintain just and honourable relations between nations; (c) foster respect for international law and treaty obligations in the dealings of organised peoples with one another; and (d) encourage settlement of international disputes by arbitration. This directive guides India's foreign policy and its commitment to multilateralism.

id: art54-56
category: The Union Executive
title: Articles 54-56 - Election, Term and Impeachment of the President
part: Part V
keywords: president, election, electoral college, term, five years, impeachment, removal, Article 61
---

The President shall be elected by the members of an electoral college consisting of (a) the elected members of both Houses of Parliament, and (b) the elected members of the Legislative Assemblies of the States and of Delhi and Puducherry. The President holds office for a term of five years and is eligible for re-election. The President may resign by writing to the Vice-President, or may be removed from office by impeachment for "violation of the Constitution." The process of impeachment requires a resolution passed by a majority of not less than two-thirds of the total membership of the House that initiates it, and then investigated by the other House. If after such investigation, a resolution is passed by a majority of not less than two-thirds of the total membership of the investigating House declaring the charges sustained, the President stands removed.

---
id: art55
category: The Union Executive
title: Article 55 - Manner of election of President — Proportional Representation
part: Part V
keywords: president election, proportional representation, single transferable vote, electoral college, value of vote
---

The election of the President shall be held in accordance with the system of proportional representation by means of the single transferable vote and the voting at such election shall be by secret ballot. As far as practicable, uniformity shall be maintained in the scale of representation of the different States at the election of the President. The value of each State MLA's vote is calculated by a formula: (Total population of state / Total number of elected MLAs) × (1/1000). The value of each MP's vote = Total value of all state MLA votes / Total number of elected MPs in both Houses.

---
id: art57-62
category: The Union Executive
title: Articles 57-62 - President: Re-election, Qualifications, Conditions, Oath and Impeachment
part: Part V
keywords: president, re-election, qualifications, age 35, citizen, oath, impeachment, violation of constitution
---

A person shall be eligible for election as President if he (a) is a citizen of India, (b) has completed the age of 35 years, (c) is qualified for election as a member of the House of the People, and (d) does not hold any office of profit under the Government of India or any State. The President shall not be a member of Parliament or State Legislature. Before entering office, the President makes an oath to preserve, protect and defend the Constitution. The President cannot be removed except by impeachment under Article 61 for "violation of the Constitution," requiring a two-thirds majority of total membership of investigating House.

---
id: art63-71
category: The Union Executive
title: Articles 63-71 - The Vice-President of India
part: Part V
keywords: vice-president, election, removal, qualifications, Rajya Sabha, chairman, acting president
---

There shall be a Vice-President of India who shall be ex officio Chairman of the Council of States (Rajya Sabha). The Vice-President is elected by members of both Houses of Parliament by proportional representation with single transferable vote. Qualifications: citizen of India, age 35+, qualified for Rajya Sabha membership. Term: 5 years. The VP can be removed by a resolution of the Rajya Sabha passed by a majority of all members and agreed to by the Lok Sabha. When the office of President falls vacant, the Vice-President acts as President. The Vice-President may also discharge the functions of the President when the President is unable to do so due to absence, illness or any other cause.

---
id: art75
category: The Union Executive
title: Article 75 - Prime Minister and Council of Ministers
part: Part V
keywords: prime minister, council of ministers, appointment, collective responsibility, confidence, Lok Sabha, cabinet
---

The Prime Minister shall be appointed by the President and other Ministers shall be appointed by the President on the advice of the Prime Minister. The total number of Ministers, including the Prime Minister, shall not exceed 15% of the total members of the House of the People (91st Amendment). The Council of Ministers shall be collectively responsible to the House of the People (Lok Sabha). A Minister who is not a member of either House for six consecutive months shall cease to be a Minister. The salaries and allowances of Ministers shall be determined by Parliament by law.

---
id: art77-78
category: The Union Executive
title: Articles 77-78 - Conduct of Government Business and PM's Duty to Communicate
part: Part V
keywords: government business, president, rules, orders, prime minister, duty, inform, communication
---

All executive action of the Government of India shall be expressed to be taken in the name of the President. The President shall make rules for the more convenient transaction of the business of the Government, and for the allocation of business among Ministers. It shall be the duty of the Prime Minister (a) to communicate to the President all decisions of the Council of Ministers relating to the administration of the Union and proposals for legislation; (b) to furnish such information relating to the administration as the President may call for; and (c) if the President so requires, to submit for the consideration of the Council of Ministers any matter on which a decision has been taken by a Minister but which has not been considered by the Council.

id: art81-84
category: Parliament
title: Articles 81-84 - Composition of the House of the People (Lok Sabha)
part: Part V
keywords: Lok Sabha, House of the People, composition, 543 members, constituencies, qualifications, age 25, term
---

The House of the People (Lok Sabha) shall consist of not more than 550 members chosen by direct election from territorial constituencies in the States (530) and not more than 20 from Union Territories. Seats are allotted to each State based on population. 84 seats are reserved for Scheduled Castes and 47 for Scheduled Tribes (as of current delimitation). Qualifications for membership: citizen of India, age 25+, and other qualifications as prescribed by Parliament. The normal life of the Lok Sabha is five years from the date of its first meeting, but may be dissolved earlier. During proclamation of Emergency, Parliament may extend the term by one year at a time.

---
id: art85-88
category: Parliament
title: Articles 85-88 - Sessions, Prorogation, Adjournment and Address by President
part: Part V
keywords: parliament session, prorogation, adjournment, budget session, monsoon session, winter session, president address, joint sitting
---

The President shall summon each House of Parliament at such time and place as he thinks fit, but six months shall not intervene between its last sitting in one session and the date appointed for its first sitting in the next session. The President may prorogue the Houses or dissolve the House of the People. The President shall address both Houses assembled together at the commencement of the first session after each general election and at the commencement of the first session of each year. Parliament typically meets in three sessions: Budget Session (Feb-May), Monsoon Session (Jul-Aug), and Winter Session (Nov-Dec).

---
id: art89-98
category: Parliament
title: Articles 89-98 - Officers of Parliament
part: Part V
keywords: speaker, deputy speaker, chairman, deputy chairman, Rajya Sabha, Lok Sabha, presiding officer, casting vote
---

The Vice-President of India is the ex officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha. The Rajya Sabha elects a Deputy Chairman from among its members. The House of the People (Lok Sabha) chooses two of its members to be the Speaker and Deputy Speaker. The Speaker presides over Lok Sabha sessions, maintains order, decides points of order, and has the casting vote in case of a tie. The Speaker can be removed by a resolution passed by a majority of all the then members of the House. While a resolution for removal is under consideration, the Speaker cannot preside.

---
id: art99-104
category: Parliament
title: Articles 99-104 - Oath, Vacating Seats, Disqualification
part: Part V
keywords: oath, MP, member of parliament, disqualification, vacation, dual membership, office of profit, anti-defection
---

Every member of Parliament shall, before taking seat, make an oath or affirmation to bear true faith and allegiance to the Constitution and to uphold sovereignty and integrity of India. Disqualifications for membership: (a) holds office of profit under Government; (b) is of unsound mind declared by court; (c) is an undischarged insolvent; (d) is not a citizen or has voluntarily acquired foreign citizenship; (e) is disqualified under any law by Parliament. The Tenth Schedule (anti-defection law) also provides for disqualification if a member voluntarily gives up membership of their party or votes contrary to party direction.

---
id: art105-106
category: Parliament
title: Articles 105-106 - Powers, Privileges and Immunities of Parliament
part: Part V
keywords: parliamentary privilege, freedom of speech, immunity, MP, members, salaries, allowances
---

Subject to the Constitution, there shall be freedom of speech in Parliament. No member of Parliament shall be liable to any proceedings in any court in respect of anything said or any vote given in Parliament or any committee thereof. No person shall be liable in respect of the publication by or under the authority of either House of Parliament of any report, paper, votes or proceedings. Members are entitled to salaries and allowances as determined by Parliament by law.

---
id: art107-111
category: Parliament
title: Articles 107-111 - Legislative Procedure — Bills, Assent, Money Bills
part: Part V
keywords: bill, legislation, ordinary bill, money bill, joint sitting, president assent, veto, legislative procedure
---

A Bill may originate in either House of Parliament (except Money Bills which can only be introduced in Lok Sabha). A Bill passed by one House is transmitted to the other House. If the other House rejects it, or more than six months elapse, the President may summon a joint sitting of both Houses. In a joint sitting, the Bill is passed by a majority of the total number of members of both Houses present and voting. When a Bill is passed by both Houses, it is presented to the President for assent. The President may (a) give assent, (b) withhold assent, or (c) return the Bill for reconsideration (except Money Bills). If the Bill is passed again by both Houses with or without amendments, the President cannot withhold assent. For Money Bills, the President may give assent or withhold but cannot return for reconsideration.

---
id: art112-117
category: Parliament
title: Articles 112-117 - Budget and Financial Provisions
part: Part V
keywords: budget, annual financial statement, consolidated fund, contingency fund, appropriation bill, finance bill, expenditure
---

The President shall cause to be laid before both Houses of Parliament an annual financial statement (Union Budget) showing estimated receipts and expenditure for that year. Expenditure is classified as "charged upon the Consolidated Fund" (non-votable, e.g., President's salary, SC judges' salary, CAG salary, debt charges) and "other expenditure" (votable). Demands for grants are processed in Lok Sabha. The Appropriation Bill authorizes withdrawal from the Consolidated Fund. No money shall be withdrawn except under appropriation made by law. The Contingency Fund of India enables the President to make advances for unforeseen expenditure pending authorization by Parliament.

---
id: art118-122
category: Parliament
title: Articles 118-122 - Rules of Procedure, No Court Challenge, Language
part: Part V
keywords: rules of procedure, parliament, courts, validity, challenge, language, Hindi, English, proceedings
---

Each House of Parliament shall make rules for regulating its procedure and conduct of business. The validity of any proceedings in Parliament shall not be called in question on the ground of any alleged irregularity of procedure. No officer or member of Parliament shall be subject to the jurisdiction of any court in respect of the exercise of powers of regulating procedure. The business of Parliament shall be transacted in Hindi or in English, with the Presiding Officer's permission for a member to address in their mother tongue.
